HISTORICAL PLACES IN WORLD






TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Agra, India, is a breathtaking white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Renowned for its exquisite architecture and intricate details, it stands as a symbol of eternal love and is considered one of the most iconic and majestic structures in the world.





RED FORT

The Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, India, is an imposing fortress built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century. Known for its red sandstone walls and intricate Mughal architecture, it served as the main residence of emperors for centuries and remains a significant historical landmark.





INDIAN GATE

India Gate, located in the heart of New Delhi, is a majestic war memorial built in honor of the soldiers who sacrificed their lives during World War I. Surrounded by lush lawns, this iconic monument stands tall as a symbol of national pride and unity in India.





LOTUS TEMPLE

The Lotus Temple, located in Delhi, India, is a striking Bahá'í House of Worship renowned for its distinctive lotus flower-inspired design. Embracing the principles of unity and equality, it welcomes people of all faiths to its serene premises for silent contemplation and prayer.





AYODYA RAM MANDIR

The Ram Mandir in Ayodhya, India, is a sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ram, whose construction began after a long-standing legal dispute was settled in 2019. Emerging as a symbol of cultural and religious significance, it stands as a testament to the beliefs and heritage of millions of devotees.





QUTUB MINOR

The Qutub Minar, situated in Delhi, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world's tallest brick minaret. Built in the 12th century, it is a remarkable example of Indo-Islamic architecture and stands as a testament to the rich historical and cultural heritage of the region.





STATUE OF UNITY

The Statue of Liberty, situated on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, is an iconic symbol of freedom and democracy. Gifted by France to the United States in 1886, this colossal statue represents the enduring spirit of liberty and welcomes visitors with its torch held high and the tablet inscribed with the date of American independence.





RASTRAPATHI BHAWAN

Rashtrapati Bhavan, the official residence of the President of India, is an architectural marvel in New Delhi. With its grand neoclassical design and sprawling Mughal Gardens, it stands as a symbol of the country's democratic governance and historic significance.
